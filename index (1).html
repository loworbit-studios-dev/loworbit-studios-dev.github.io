About the Game

The World Has Already Ended.
Now We Rebuild.

Vaeltharr was a continent of rival kingdoms bound under fragile peace — until the Undying Compact performed the Sundering Ritual and shattered the boundary between the living world and the Hollow Below. The Hollow Plague spread. The great cities fell. The dead rose.

You are what remains. A lord of ruins, a people without a home, a stronghold barely standing. This is where you begin.

Genre Real-Time Gothic Strategy / MMO
Platform Mobile — iOS & Android
Monetisation Free to Play — cosmetics & convenience only. Never pay-for-power.
Studio Low Orbit Studios — independent, solo development
Status Early Development — Pre-Alpha

Development Roadmap

The Road Ahead

A living document. Subject to change as development evolves — but this is where we're going.

Phase 0 — Foundation

Game Design Document & Community Setup

Core systems designed on paper. World, lore, building districts, combat, Order mechanics, ethical monetisation framework, and the community forum all established.

Complete

Phase 1 — Vertical Slice

Single-Screen City Builder

A working prototype: one stronghold, resource ticks, building placement, basic UI. Ships to a real device. Proves the pipeline end-to-end before complex systems are built.

In Progress

Phase 2 — Core Loop

Research, Training & Marching

Research tree, troop training queues, march system with up to 7 simultaneous marches, Rally Point, and basic world map presence.

Upcoming

Phase 3 — Social Layer

Order System & Realm

Order Vault, Order chat, Realm chat, moderation tools, and march coordination between Order members.

Upcoming

Phase 4 — Combat

PvP, Wall Defences & Campaign

Full combat resolution, tiered wall defences, Marshall assignment system, and the Campaign map with stage-based PvE progression.

Upcoming

Phase 5 — Beta

Closed Beta — First Realm

First real players in a real realm. 250-player cap. Community feedback drives final balancing before soft launch.

Bug Reports

Found Something Broken?

Bug reports are handled through GitHub Issues. Every report is read, triaged, and tracked. Swift resolution is a commitment, not a promise.

Before submitting, check whether the bug has already been reported. If it has, add a comment with any additional detail — duplicate reports with extra information are still useful.
  1. 01 Go to the Issues page on GitHub.
  2. 02 Search existing issues to check your bug hasn't already been reported.
  3. 03 Click New Issue and describe: what happened, what you expected to happen, and how to reproduce it.
  4. 04 Include your device, OS version, and realm name if relevant. Screenshots or recordings are always helpful.
